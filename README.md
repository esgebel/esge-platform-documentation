Documentation for Institution Admins, Mentors and Mentees.

## Introduction

The Online Scoring Platform (OSP) is a modern web application developed by [ESGE Academy](https://esge.org) to enable participants to manage their activities related to the [GESEA](https://gesea.eu) educational programme.

There are two main activities that can be managed through the platform: **training sessions** and **certification sessions**.

In general there are three types of participants on the platform:

- **Institutions** (represented by their Admins) are centers that facilitate training and/or certification sessions. If you are an Institution Admin you will find the pages below [Institutions](/esge-platform-documentation/pages/institutions.html) most useful.

- **Mentors** are medical professionals that supervise training and/or certification sessions. If you are a Mentor you will find the pages below [Mentors](/esge-platform-documentation/pages/mentors.html) most useful.

- **Mentees** are medical professionals that seek certification. They can prepare for their certification by following training sessions. If you are a Mentee you will find the pages below [Mentees](/esge-platform-documentation/pages/mentees.html) most useful.

## Email Account

To use the platform it is advised to always use an individual account. Sharing accounts and passwords is **strongly** discouraged.

Under the circumstance where you are not able to use an existing email address to create an account on the platform, consider creating a free email address using [Proton Mail](https://account.proton.me/mail/signup?plan=free). Proton Mail is a European provider of email with a userfriendly web interface and strong security.

The reasons why we discourage sharing accounts and passwords:

- If multiple people have access to the same account, the chance increases that this account get leaked.
- If multiple people have access to the same account and 1 person leaves the organization, then you must either reset the password for everyone *or* the person leaving will still have access to the shared account.
- If multiple people have access to the same account it is impossible to determine who did what, which can be useful when someone made a mistake (for example accidental deletion of data).

More in-depth information on this topic can be found in this [Techradar article](https://www.techradar.com/news/the-dangers-of-password-sharing-at-work).
